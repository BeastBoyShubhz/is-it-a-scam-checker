3,000 people reported receiving suspicious emails with links to fake login pages last week. What's going on? 
## How This Scam Works 
It starts with an email claiming your account's been compromised, asking you to verify your login details. Don't. It's a trap. They're after your login credentials and personal data. 
## Who Is Being Targeted 
Young adults, mainly students and freelancers, are being targeted on social media platforms and online forums. Can't they see it's a scam? 
## Red Flags to Watch For 
🚩 Unsolicited emails with generic greetings 
🚩 Requests for sensitive info via email or phone 
🚩 Links to suspicious websites or login pages 
🚩 Poor grammar and spelling in official-looking emails 
🚩 Threats to suspend or delete your account if you don't comply 
## What to Do If You've Been Hit 
1. Change your passwords immediately 
2. Enable two-factor authentication 
3. Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity 
4. Report the incident to the relevant authorities 
5. Consider using a password manager
