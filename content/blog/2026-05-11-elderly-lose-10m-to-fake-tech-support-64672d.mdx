£8.2 million was lost by over 3,500 UK seniors in the last quarter to fake tech support scams. It's a staggering number that's only rising. What's behind this surge in scams targeting the elderly?

## How This Scam Works
The scam starts with a call from someone claiming to be from a well-known tech company, saying your computer has a virus. They'll try to convince you to let them access your computer remotely to fix the problem. Once they're in, they'll steal your personal info and money. It's that simple, yet it's working on thousands of people.

## Who Is Being Targeted
The elderly are the primary targets here, but why? It's not just because they might be less tech-savvy. Scammers know seniors often have a nest egg, and they're more likely to trust someone who claims to be from a reputable company. The question is, can't we do more to protect this vulnerable group?

## What to Do If You've Been Hit
If you think you've fallen victim to one of these scams, act fast. First, contact your bank to freeze your accounts. Then, report the incident to the relevant authorities. Finally, consider seeking advice from a cybersecurity expert to secure your computer and personal data.

## Red Flags to Watch For
- 🚩 Unsolicited calls claiming to be from tech companies
- 🚩 Requests for remote access to your computer
- 🚩 Demands for payment in gift cards or wire transfers
- 🚩 Threats to arrest you if you don't comply
- 🚩 Grammar and spelling mistakes in communications
- 🚩 Pressure to act immediately, without giving you time to think
